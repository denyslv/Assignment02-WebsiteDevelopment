Minecraft Volume Beta

Minecraft Volume Beta
2013

Composed by C418

Genre: Ambient, Electronic

Minecraft Volume Beta is the second official soundtrack album for Minecraft, composed by C418. It features ambient and electronic music that perfectly captures the game's atmosphere and exploration themes.

Featured Tracks

1. "Ki" (01:32)
An introduction to the album, dark and foreboding, unlike its predecessor "Key" from Volume Alpha.
2. "Alpha" (10:03)
A medley of past songs that plays when you "beat" Minecraft, celebrating the music from Volume Alpha.
3. "Dead Voxel" (04:56)
A haunting track that captures the eerie atmosphere of Minecraft's underground.
4. "Blind Spots" (05:32)
Created for Minecraft's creative mode, featuring a constant remixing of its core theme with melancholic undertones.
5. "Flake" (02:50)
A delicate piece that evokes the gentle falling of snow in Minecraft's winter biomes.
6. "Moog City 2" (03:00)
A recreation of "Moog City" from Minecraft Alpha, this time using actual Moog synthesizers and other synths.
7. "Concrete Halls" (04:14)
A track that captures the industrial atmosphere of Minecraft's strongholds.
8. "Biome Fest" (06:18)
A minimalist piece created for Minecraft Alpha, showcasing the beauty of achieving tones with few notes.
9. "Mutation" (03:05)
A track that explores the darker side of Minecraft's world.
10. "Haunt Muskie" (06:01)
An anagram for Hatsune Miku, inspired by nostalgic memories of an old puzzle game about fixing rollercoasters.
11. "Warmth" (03:59)
The Nether's theme, exploring the idea that even hell isn't all bad, though it remains a harsh environment.
12. "Floating Trees" (04:04)
A peaceful track that captures the serenity of Minecraft's floating islands.
13. "Aria Math" (05:10)
Features Pan Drums and old synthesizers, inspired by the awe-inspiring creations in Minecraft's creative mode.
14. "Kyoto" (04:09)
A track that blends traditional Japanese elements with electronic music.
15. "Ballad of the Cats" (04:35)
A playful track dedicated to Minecraft's feline companions.
16. "Taswell" (08:35)
A farewell to a friend, focusing on the happiness they showed in life rather than their passing.
17. "Beginning 2" (02:56)
Similar to "Beginning" on Alpha, marking either the end of the album or a new beginning.
18. "Dreiton" (08:17)
An 8-minute piece about embracing minimalism and the awe of creativity in Minecraft.
19. "The End" (15:04)
A 15-minute piece for Minecraft's final stage, containing references to most of Volume Alpha's songs.
20. "Chirp" (03:06)
Part of the collectible records in Minecraft.
21. "Wait" (03:54)
A track that captures the anticipation of waiting in Minecraft's world.
22. "Mellohi" (01:38)
Another collectible record, known for its melancholic tone.
23. "Stal" (02:32)
A track that captures the underground atmosphere of Minecraft's caves.
24. "Strad" (03:08)
A peaceful track that plays in Minecraft's overworld.
25. "Eleven" (01:11)
A short, mysterious track that adds to Minecraft's ambient atmosphere.
26. "Ward" (04:10)
A track that captures the protective nature of Minecraft's world.
27. "Mall" (03:18)
A track that evokes the feeling of exploring Minecraft's structures.
28. "Blocks" (05:43)
A track that celebrates the building blocks of Minecraft's world.
29. "Far" (03:12)
A track that captures the feeling of distant exploration in Minecraft.
30. "Intro" (04:36)
An improvisational piece serving as both a goodbye and a hello.
